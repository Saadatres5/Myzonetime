# MyZoneTime SEO, AEO, GEO Audit

## Summary
MyZoneTime already has strong on-page fundamentals: route-level SEO, structured data, FAQ schema, canonical tags, and a large content surface around world clock and time tools. The biggest opportunity is to make the homepage and core tool pages more directly match the search intent that competitor sites are winning on: world clock, time zone converter, meeting planner, and time difference calculator.

## Competitor positioning scan
Based on public positioning from market leaders such as World Time Buddy and broader time-tool competitors, the strongest keyword clusters are:

- world clock
- time zone converter
- meeting planner
- online meeting scheduler
- time difference calculator
- convert time zones
- world clock for cities
- business hours across time zones
- travel time and timezone comparison

## What MyZoneTime already does well
- Broad coverage of city and timezone pages
- Strong tool-specific pages for converters, calculators, and planners
- FAQ and schema markup for answer-style search visibility
- Category pages for city/timezone/time-difference intent
- Large internal linking surface across tools and city pages

## What to improve next
1. Strengthen homepage copy around high-intent phrases
2. Add answer-style content for “what is a world clock?” and “how do I convert time zones?”
3. Keep building city and timezone pages with intent-driven FAQs
4. Expand internal links from high-traffic tool pages to related tools
5. Create a sustained backlink outreach plan with quality, relevance, and trust signals

## Legitimate backlink opportunities
Use a mix of editorial, directory, and community links rather than spammy automated links.

- Local and global business blogs that publish productivity tools
- Remote work and distributed team resource pages
- Travel blogs that mention time zones and travel planning
- Developer blogs that link to Unix time, date, and timezone utilities
- Education and student resource pages for timezone calculators
- Productivity and calendar tool directories
- Community forums and Q&A sites with useful, non-promotional answers
- Press releases or brand mentions when launching new time tools

## Suggested outreach workflow
1. Create a list of 30–50 relevant sites in the productivity, travel, remote work, and developer niches.
2. Pitch a useful resource: “free world clock and time zone converter for remote teams”.
3. Offer a guest post, tool mention, or data-backed guide.
4. Keep anchors natural: world clock, time zone converter, meeting planner, time difference calculator.
5. Track wins in a simple spreadsheet with URL, domain authority, anchor text, and status.

## Immediate action items
- Keep the homepage keyword-rich and answer-focused
- Add more “how to” content to the converter and planner pages
- Publish short comparison and use-case pages such as:
  - how to convert time zones
  - how to schedule meetings across time zones
  - time difference between New York and London
  - best time to call Dubai from the US
- Continue building city pages with FAQs and local context
